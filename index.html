Wind Turbine Engineering Project

Engineering Design and Electrical Output Analysis

Project Summary

Our wind turbine project focused on designing and testing a small-scale wind turbine to determine how different design choices affect electrical power generation. Throughout the project we experimented with blade count, blade angle, and blade material to determine the optimal configuration. Voltage generated by the turbine was measured using an Arduino connected to a resistor circuit, and the results were analyzed using Matlab to determine power output. These experiments helped us determine the configurations that maximize turbine efficiency and electrical generation.

Our Team

This project was completed by our engineering team through collaboration, experimentation, and analysis of turbine performance. Each team member contributed to turbine design, data collection, and analysis of electrical output.

Best Turbine Design

Our final turbine design used three blades with a blade angle optimized to capture wind energy efficiently. Adjustments to blade material and shape improved aerodynamic performance and rotational velocity.

Final wind turbine prototype used during testing.

Arduino Voltage Measurement Setup

To measure electrical output, the turbine generator was connected to a resistor and Arduino microcontroller. Voltage readings were taken from the Arduino analog input and recorded over many samples for analysis.

Arduino and generator circuit used to record turbine voltage.

Voltage Graph (100Ω Resistor)

Voltage data collected from the Arduino was exported into Matlab, where voltage values were plotted and used to calculate electrical power output.

Matlab graph showing voltage output over time.

How Power Was Measured

Electrical power output was determined using the equation P = V² / R, where V is voltage measured by the Arduino and R is the resistance in ohms. The Matlab program collected hundreds of voltage samples and calculated the average power produced by the turbine under different loads.

Key Experimental Findings

  • Increasing the number of blades increased turbine speed and power output.
  • The optimal blade angle was approximately 20°, producing the highest rotational velocity.
  • Balsa wood blades performed better than cardboard blades due to improved aerodynamics.
  • Resistance testing showed power increased with higher resistance values.
